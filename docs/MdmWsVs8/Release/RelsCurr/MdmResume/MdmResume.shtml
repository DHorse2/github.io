Resumes, CV's and Writing

Resume writing is a good area to apply writing skills. For example, you are addressing a skilled and difficult audience. It employs sales and marketing skills requiring the author to be clear and concise. The IT resume here is unfortunately a good example of not being nearly clear and concise enough (a revision is in the works.)

It might be folly to call resume creation creative writing here, yet that is precisely what it is. That statement will be accepted by many readers but likely be taken as a euphemism for creative distortion by others. The readership will include varied roles, levels of responsibility and size of organization.

An effective resume requires entegrity, accuracy and honesty. It includes the need to balance distracting and irrelevant detail from points of direct interest to the reader.

With a diverse background the different targeted resumes shown here may seem to discuss different people or be written by different authors but that is not the case. They are based solely on my own 30 year career.

I have always felt resume writing has many of the same characteristics that bother me about statistics. Its all in the presentation... Nothing short of a face to face interview and at least a brief look at a person's work really gives minimally accurate idea their true abilities and personality.
